Feats
The Legend of Draco

Draco the constellation represents Ladon - the great dragon who guarded the golden apple tree in the garden of the Hesperides. Hera had received the tree as a wedding gift from Zeus, and she planted it on the slopes of Mount Atlas, setting the daughters of Atlas to watch over it. But the Hesperides proved untrustworthy and kept picking the apples themselves. Sterner measures were required.

Hera placed the dragon Ladon around the tree. He coiled there faithfully, never sleeping, never faltering, protecting what he had been entrusted to protect.

When Heracles came to steal the golden apples as one of his twelve labors, Ladon fell defending the garden. He did not flee. He did not yield. He held his post to the end.

"Hera placed the image of the dragon in the sky as the constellation Draco, to honor his faithful service."

Bryk doesn't care about power, glory, or Legacy. He feels that being a dragon is the greatest existence there can be and wants more than anything to become one. There's something zen about his life: he's perfectly content with where he is right now because he loves the thrill of the adventure and is confident he will achieve dragonhood in time. His gaze is always locked at true north: the constellation of Draco.

Backstory

Bryk is a red dragonborn who was found as a baby sitting contentedly in the family hearth, roasting happily among the bricks. His soon-to-be parents didn't panic - he was clearly fine, clearly delighted - so they fished him out, named him for where they found him, and raised him as their own. He grew up in the forest, more at home among animals and open fire than among people, and somewhere along the way he came to believe, with complete certainty, that he is destined to become a real dragon, like Ladon of the old stories, who guarded the golden apples so faithfully that the gods set him in the stars forever.

In the same way a child will obsess over becoming an astronaut one day, Bryk has molded his entire personality around the ideals of dragonhood. He idolizes the great beasts of legend. He believes dragons are the keepers of peace and use their majesty, wisdom, and strength to protect the little guy. He loves the simple things in life - a quiet forest, family, animals.

He met a certain druid in the forest, drawn to the same wild places, and has counted her a friend ever since, entirely unaware that his unique kinship with the animals of the forest is the sort of thing that gets the attention of the goddess of the hunt.

Bryk does not know what the path to becoming a dragon looks like. He only knows he is on it.

DM Hooks
Dream
The Ascension
Bryk believes there's a path to becoming a real dragon. He doesn't know what it is but is determined to go the distance.
Bond
The Druid in the Woods
He knows Cheyenne's Artemis druid from the forest. Animals trust him without reason - possibly the goddess of wild things has noticed.
Flaw
Caught up in the role-play
He spends so much of his time practicing being a dragon, he forgets he isn't actually one yet: self-preservation and danger sense aren't as strong as they should be. In ever situation, he asks 'what would a dragon do' which frequently gets him in trouble.
Dragon 'wisdom'
Bryk knows dragons are intelligent and wise. He is neither. He thinks wise decisions and simple decisions are the same thing. When presented with an obstacle, he will generally use brute force or the first thought that comes to mind.
Joy
At Home in Fire
Fire is comfort, not danger. He sits in campfires. Enemy fireballs feel pleasant. He shares food, moves boulders for foxes, and keeps a journal of his progress toward dragonhood.
